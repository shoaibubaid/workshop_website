Physical Security and AI Workshop 2025


  • This workshop offers an in-depth understanding of the emerging hardware-level attacks on cryptosystems and AI models. Topics include side-channel attacks, fault attacks, tampering attacks on Post-Quantum Cryptography and AI models, and also application of AI to enhance or prevent such attacks. Participants will explore both offensive and defensive approaches in securing AI models, smart devices, and critical infrastructures.

    • Our Goal

    • This workshop is aimed at undergraduate and postgraduate students, researchers, and enthusiasts interested in hardware security, AI security, or the intersection of both. By attending, participants will:

    • ➤ Understand the fundamentals of physical security in computing systems.
    • ➤ Explore AI-enhanced side-channel attacks.
    • ➤ Examine real-world case studies of attacks on hardware and AI systems.
    • ➤ Learn best practices and preventive measures for securing devices and AI models and Post Quantum Cryptography.
    • ➤ Explore security vulnerabilities and protection techniques in Internet of Things (IoT) devices.
    • ➤ Gain hands-on experience with tools and techniques used in hardware and AI security research.

    • We look forward to engaging with curious minds, fostering discussions, and equipping participants with the knowledge and skills to address the emerging challenges in physical and AI security.     Frequently Asked

    Physical security involves protecting hardware and cryptographic devices from tampering, side-channel attacks, extraction of sensitive data by implementing secure enclosures, countermeasures, and tamper-detection mechanisms.
    Common attacks include skimming/cloning, side-channel analysis (power/timing), fault injection, social engineering to obtain PINs, and hardware tampering to extract keys.
    Use strong device authentication, encrypted communication, secure firmware updates, network segmentation, minimal exposed services, and regular vulnerability monitoring/patching.
